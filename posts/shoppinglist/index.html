Shopping List

Dec 10, 2023 

Inspired by Consumer Surplus, I thought it would be fun to suggest some things that I think add significant value to my life.

Health

Omega 3

I eat a vegetarian diet 99% of the time, so while I get a lot of Omega 3 ALA from olive oil (which I cook with exclusively) and nuts, I like to supplement algae oil to maintain the proper ratios of EPA and DHA too. Many vegan supplements only have DHA but this brand has both as well as antioxidant astaxanthin.

Vitamin D

Autumn/winter are my favourite seasons. To enjoy them to their fullest in our blessed UK climate, I follow the public health advice to supplement Vitamin D. It’s quite cheap at any supermarket and better than deficiency.

Tretinoin

Tretinoin is my retinoid of choice as it ticks the boxes I think I need: anti-acne (I have bad skin), anti-hyperpigmentation (I have scars from bad skin), and anti-ageing (we are all getting older).

I order from All Day Chemist online, which is much cheaper than any UK-based prescriber because it ships the generic brands readily available abroad. I spent $20 with $20 shipping for 3 tubes of Retin-A 0.025% that should last years; one month of Dermatica costs £24.99.

Hydrocolloid Patches

These sometimes work incredibly well to get rid of spots quickly, and that’s all I can ask – they’re better than nothing. This brand is what I have currently.

Kitchen

Rice Cooker

Making rice in a pan is annoying. A rice cooker with a decent capacity (ours is about 1 litre) can change your life. Often they’ll work for porridge too but I don’t make it that way.

If I get home from work and have nothing lined up, I’ll often do a one pot rice cooker meal – white or brown rice, cook, towards the end add frozen peas and kidney beans, spices, soy sauce, extra virgin olive oil, seasoning – which is done with a minimum of effort in 40 minutes.

I use my flatmate’s Tefal model from Hong Kong. I donated my Lakeland one to my mum. It’s good, but for the price it is now (£45) you could perhaps get a larger one.

Stainless Steel Pan

I enjoy having a frying pan that you can put into the oven and the dishwasher. I own this make from Kuhn Rikon which I bought from Bradbeers in Salisbury for less than the online price. For eggs, I’d probably still use non-stick to avoid the hassle.

I’m interested in cast iron, which you can make non-stick with some work, but I’m doubtful I’d use it enough to justify even a £20 model.

Bamboo Chopping Board

Something about the knife-cut grooves on the plastic boards gets under my skin. I decided it was worth the switch for the sake of my knives and my body to buy a bamboo chopping board. They’re under £7 at Tesco.

Glass Food Storage

IKEA’s glass containers are strong, dishwasher safe, and can go straight from fridge/freezer to microwave without any damage. I use the plastic lids now since I usually keep runny dishes like leftover chilli, but you can get bamboo lids too.

Home

Dehumidifier

Dehumidifiers are really helpful in modern Britain (i.e. heating is expensive and you need to avoid mould somehow).

Without the dehumidifier, relative humidity reaches 65-75% in my flat when trying to dry laundry. The dehumidifier cuts this to 50% and I can use the water for my plants.

I wouldn’t waste money on some low-capacity, no-name brand from Amazon. Meaco came out ahead in my research and I would recommend their ABC range.

Life

Library Membership

Public libraries offer such an amazing range of services for free. Request new releases and chances are they’ll get them for you too.

Safety Razor

I bought this safety razor in 2021 and found it so much better to shave with than my plastic cartridge razors. It’s also more environmentally friendly (blades are recyclable).

Glass Bottle

Chilly’s bottles are great and I will likely get a new one soon (I left my previous one at airport security). But I’ve been pleasantly surprised by how easy it is to reuse a BOL power shake bottle.

For £2.50, including a nice vegan milkshake, it has yet to shatter on me and can be cleaned in the dishwasher, which is far more convenient than insulated stainless steel bottles.
