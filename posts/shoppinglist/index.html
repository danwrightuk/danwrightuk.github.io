Not Consumer Surplus

Dec 10, 2023 

Inspired by Consumer Surplus, I thought it would be fun to suggest some economical things that I think add significant value to my life.

I know this is quite weird, but at the same time, I find it interesting to learn from other people’s experiences so I figured I might as well share my own. Personal finance is one case where I’ve definitely benefited from the research done by others.

Life

Library Membership

Public libraries offer an amazing range of services for free. Request new releases and chances are they’ll get them for you too. The thing is, you need to learn to share; I asked mine for The Blazing World but have yet to read it as someone else got in there first.

Password Manager

I won’t say which service I use, but a good password manager is well worth the money for the security and peace of mind. To me, this is the epitome of good value for money.

VPN

Some YouTuber, somewhere, is being sponsored by NordVPN or Surfshark to where you can get a year or two for basically nothing. If you’re going to use a network or website anyway that you consider to be at all dodgy, you might as well use a VPN.

Kitchen

Rice Cooker

One of the most underrated modern conveniences. If I get home from work and have nothing planned, I can do a one pot rice cooker meal – white or brown rice, towards the end add frozen peas and kidney beans, spices, soy sauce, extra virgin olive oil, spring onion – which is done with a minimum of effort in 45 minutes.

I use my flatmate’s Tefal model from Hong Kong. I had a Lakeland model which I gave to someone else; for the price it is now (£45) you could perhaps get a larger one.

Stainless Steel Pan

Realistically, I bought one because my favourite food YouTuber uses them, but they are useful for being oven and dishwasher safe. I own this make from Kuhn Rikon which I bought from Bradbeers in Salisbury (virtue signalling about how I support the high street).

Bamboo Chopping Board

Do grooves left by knives on plastic cutting boards suggest microplastics are winding up in your food? I’m not sure but I decided I’d buy a bamboo chopping board just in case. They’re literally under £7 at Tesco.

Glass Food Storage

IKEA’s glass containers are strong, dishwasher safe, and can go straight from fridge/freezer to microwave without any damage. I use the plastic lids now since I usually keep runny dishes like leftover chilli, but you can get bamboo lids too.

Home

Dehumidifier

Dehumidifiers are really helpful in modern Britain (i.e. heating is expensive but you need to avoid mould somehow). I like lüften but opening the windows for long stretches (e.)

Without the dehumidifier, relative humidity reaches 65-75% in my flat. The dehumidifier cuts this to 50% and I can use the water for my plants.

I wouldn’t waste money on a low-capacity, no-name brand from Amazon. Meaco came out ahead in my research and I would recommend their ABC range.

Air Purifier

Living near a big road in London, having my air purifier – a Levoit Core 300S, which is powerful enough for my room – reassures me that I am doing something to limit the effects of toxic air getting into my home. Outside the home, we need a political solution…

Health

Omega 3

For people who attempt vegetarianism/veganism, it’s worth supplementing DHA/ELA Many vegan supplements only have DHA. This brand has both as well as antioxidant astaxanthin.

Vitamin D

Besides Omega 3, Vitamin D is the only other thing I consider it worthwhile supplementing regularly and one of the few things the NHS recommends.

Tretinoin

For whatever reason I suffer adult acne – an unsympathetic and unsexy problem to have – so tretinoin is my retinoid of choice.

I order from All Day Chemist online, which is much cheaper than any UK-based prescriber because it ships the generic brands available abroad.
