Winter of this Content

Apr 12, 2023 

I wanted to make a quick note of some of the better things I’ve read, seen, or listened to in the first few months of 2023 – especially the new releases. I’ve tried to limit myself to one recommendation and one honourable mention in each case.

Film

Puss in Boots: The Last Wish is my favourite release of the year so far. All of the elements come together brilliantly: vivid animation, fantastic characters, genuinely funny moments, and a surprisingly mature story that makes it feel like the Logan of the Shrek franchise. I have recommended it to people before, but I’m not sure how seriously they took that.

It’s not a 2023 film, but I’ll give an honourable mention to The Banshees of Inisherin. I watched it on a flight to Miami to get me in the partying mood. It’s an unflashy but profound film that in any other year would’ve come away with an Oscar.

Music

A new release I’ve listened to extensively has been Caroline Polachek’s Desire, I Want to Turn Into You, with 860 minutes streamed so far. I do really like the album, but I’ll be curious to see how many tracks I’m still listening to by the year’s end.

My main shout-out goes to Miss Anthropocene by Grimes, which is my top album of the year to date with 377 streams (Art Angels sits at 349 so it’s been a Grimes-y year). I am the #91 listener of You’ll miss me when I’m not around on Spotify and I expect to keep rising up the ranks as it still goes hard.

Literature

The best book I’ve read this year has been The Road to Wigan Pier by George Orwell. I could say a lot more, but suffice to say it’s a really compelling read with such relevance to modern Britain (generally for reasons which reflect poorly on our politics and culture).

I have yet to read anything published in 2023, but Lambeth Libraries have kindly agreed to get me a copy of The Blazing World: A New History of Revolutionary England by Jonathan Healey.

TV

Chris Packham’s Inside Our Autistic Minds was wonderful in concept and execution: an autistic presenter platforming autistic people. I’ve spoken about this show with friends and family, and they all shared my positivity.

I will also give quick mentions to Fight the Power: How Hip Hop Changed the World and The Last of Us.
